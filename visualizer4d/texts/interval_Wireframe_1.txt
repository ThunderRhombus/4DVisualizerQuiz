In this model, the fourth dimension coordinate (w to the normal x, y, and z) is reflected in the deformation of the normal x, y, and z coordinates, and in the coloring of the lines.

Raising the orthogonality (Ctrl + Scroll) will push points with lower, or negative w values inward, and push points with higher, or positive w values outward. Points with positive w values gain a redder hue and points with lower w values gain a bluer hue.

Setting orthogonality to 0 will let you see where the vertices actually are in 3D space.

The question is asking you to describe the 4D rotations of this object, so keep track of how the shape changes over time, paying special attention to how the shape’s edges and vertices begin (R to reset) and how they take their first steps (Space to pause, Neutral scroll to rotate in 4D).
